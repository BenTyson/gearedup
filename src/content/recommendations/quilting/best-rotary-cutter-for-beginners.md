faqs:
  - question: "What size rotary cutter should a beginner get?"
    answer: "45mm is the standard size and best for beginners. It handles most fabrics well, works with standard cutting mats, and replacement blades are widely available and affordable. Once you're comfortable, you might add a 28mm for curves or 60mm for thick materials."

  - question: "How often should I replace rotary cutter blades?"
    answer: "Replace blades when you notice dragging, skipping, or when you need extra pressure to cut through fabric. For regular quilters, this typically means every 8-12 projects. A dull blade is more dangerous than a sharp one because you'll press harder and risk slipping."

  - question: "Are rotary cutters dangerous?"
    answer: "Yes, rotary cutters are extremely sharp and require careful handling. Always engage the safety lock when not actively cutting, cut away from your body, use a proper cutting mat, and keep cutters out of reach of children. With proper technique, they're safe to use."

  - question: "Can I use a rotary cutter without a cutting mat?"
    answer: "No, always use a self-healing cutting mat. It protects your work surface, extends blade life significantly, and provides measurement guides for accurate cuts. Never cut on bare tables, cardboard, or other surfaces."

  - question: "Why do quilters prefer rotary cutters over scissors?"
    answer: "Rotary cutters are faster (cut multiple layers at once), more accurate (use with rulers for perfectly straight lines), and less tiring for large projects. They're essential for precision piecing. Scissors still have their place for detail work and trimming threads."
